**making an [[RNA]] copy from a gene’s DNA sequence** (this step is called [[transcription]]). The process by which information from a gene is transformed into a product. 

-If a gene is “**on**” in a cell → it is being transcribed → you get RNA made from that gene.

Turned "on" always means expressed But just because a [[protein-coding gene]] exists doesnt'tmean that its expressed.

-For **protein-coding genes**, that RNA is mRNA, and it can then be **translated** into a **protein**.

**DNA (gene) → RNA** is already “expression.”  
**RNA → protein** is the extra step for _protein-coding_ genes.

The genes that are not expressed its because the cell doesn't need them, like for example liver cells and neurons have almost the same DNA but have different [[transcription factors]] and [[gene regulation]] that are active because they express different genes.
