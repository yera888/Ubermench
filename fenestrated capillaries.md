## 2. Fenestrated capillaries

These have **small pores/windows** in the endothelial cells.

- “Fenestrated” basically means **windowed**
    
- The basement membrane is still continuous
    
- They allow **more fluid and small dissolved substances** to pass than continuous capillaries
    

**Found in:** small intestine, ciliary process of the eye, many endocrine glands, kidneys

### Easy example

Imagine a screen with **tiny holes punched in it**.  
More water and small particles can move through.

These are useful where the body needs **a lot of exchange quickly**, like:

- **kidneys** filtering blood
    
- **small intestine** absorbing nutrients
