What the heart is enclosed by so that it doesn't move too much or overfill with blood.

There are 3 different kinds of pericardium .

---
# Outer part of pericardium
### Fibrous pericardium
dense connective tissue -> fibrous pericardium 

## Inner part of pericardium 
#### Serious pericardium
double layered serous membrane 

Fibrous sac + serious lining = pericardial sac
###### Parietal layer 

Pericardial cavity = the space in between these layers 

###### Visceral layer = epicardium 

These 2 layers secrete lubricating serious fluid to decrease friction when the heart beats and the layers rub against each other it can become a real layer if the amount of fluid increased due to inflammation.

# Heart wall
###### Epicardium 
Visceral layer of the serous pericardium more fat gets deposited here so the layer becomes more fatty (outer layer of the heart) 

### Myocardium 
Middle layer of the heart made of cardiac muscle tissue it is the thickest. Generates the force to pump blood. It gets bigger when the arteries narrow because the heart has to work harder to work.

### Endocardium 
internal surface of the heart chambers and external surface of the valves.
