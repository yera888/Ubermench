all the DNA in the nucleus of a cell 

Humans have about **3 billion DNA letters (base pairs)** in the genome (per haploid set), **not 3 billion genes**.

Human genome: 20,000 genes. 

- **Genome size** ≈ 3,000,000,000 base pairs
    
- **Protein-coding genes** ≈ ~20,000 (ballpark)
    
- Plus **many non–protein-coding genes** (genes that make functional RNAs, like rRNAs, tRNAs, many regulatory RNAs), plus **pseudogenes**, plus lots of regulatory DNA, repeats, mobile elements, etc.
