This is when the RNA does the job itself like:



- **rRNA** (ribosome core) - Machine part
- mRNA - Message
    
- **tRNA** (adaptor for translation) - Adapter
    
- **snRNA / snoRNA** (RNA processing) - Processing tool 
    
- **miRNA / siRNA** (gene silencing) - Regulator
    
- many **lncRNAs** (regulation/scaffolding; some have clear functions) - Regulator


These can:

- **Build machines:** rRNA forms the ribosome’s core; it’s part of the protein-making machine.
    
- **Carry/translate information:** tRNA helps convert the mRNA “code” into amino acids.
    
- **Process other RNAs:** snRNA splices mRNA; snoRNA modifies rRNA.
    
- **Regulate genes:** miRNA/siRNA can silence genes by blocking or degrading mRNAs.
    
- **Control chromosome/gene activity:** many lncRNAs affect which genes are turned on/off by shaping chromatin or recruiting regulatory proteins.
