The smallest ones only have endothelium surrounded by a single layer of smooth muscle vasoconstriction here raises blood pressure and vasocontraction decreases it into the capillaries.

- When the smooth muscle **tightens**, the arteriole gets **narrower**.  
    This is called **vasoconstriction**.
    
    - Less blood can pass through
        
    - Less blood reaches the capillaries
        
    - Blood pressure goes up
        
- When the smooth muscle **relaxes**, the arteriole gets **wider**.  
    This is called **vasodilation**.
    
    - More blood can pass through
        
    - More blood reaches the capillaries

- **narrow lumen** = less blood flow
    
- **wide lumen** = more blood flow
### What “sympathetic innervation” means

This just means **nerves from the sympathetic nervous system** send signals to the arteriole.

Usually this system is active when you are stressed, scared, or exercising. It often tells the smooth muscle to **contract**, which narrows the arteriole.
**Arterioles control how much blood gets into the capillaries by narrowing or widening.**
