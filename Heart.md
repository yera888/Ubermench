The heart connects to [[blood vessels]] that transport blood between the heart and all body systems. One side pumps blood to the lungs and the other to the body tissues. It maintains blood pressure which is the force the blood is pushing against the inside of the vessel walls . 

The arteries and veins that enter and leave the heart are called the great vessels because there usually very large. 

- normal heart beat is 75 pm
Blood pumped from one ventricle per minute -> cardiac output.
[[Valves]] ensure unidirectional flow.
When body is more active it needs more oxygen and nutrients delivered at a faster pace so it increases output 5 or 6 fold.
