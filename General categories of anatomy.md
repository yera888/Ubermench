
Microscopic Anatomy
--
	
Examining structures that cannot be seen through the unaided eye.

- cytology (cell - study) The study of cellular anatomy which is the study of single body cells and their internal structures. 
- histology (web - study) The study of tissues; Histology takes a wider approach to microscopic anatomy by examining how groups of specialized cells and their products function for a common purpose. 

Macroscopic / Gross Anatomy 
--

Investigates the structures & relationships of large body parts that are visible to the unaided eye. In these investigations they keep preserved species or their parts to cut open and examine. 

**So is gross that they cut open these body parts but that is GROSS or MICROSCOPIC anatomy**

Magnetic Resonance Imaging 
-- 

Also known as an MRI, noninvasive technique to visualize SOFT TISSUE. Placed in a large electromagnetic chamber where the magnet generates a strong magnetic field that causes protons which are hydrogen atoms in the tissue to align. After the exposure to the RADIO WAVES the protons absorb additional energy and align in a different direction then they realign themselves to the magnetic field after the radio waves are turned off. This results in the release of the atoms excess energy at different rates depending on the type of tissue.
