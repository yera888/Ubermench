After the process, the resulting strand of mRNA contains only information from the gene’s exons. 

**RNA splicing** is the general process. [[Alternative splicing]] is a special case of it.

###### You start with **pre-mRNA** (the first RNA copy made from DNA).
    
- It contains **exons** (kept) and **introns** (removed).
    
- **Splicing** cuts out the introns and stitches exons together to make **one mature mRNA**.
    

So: **splicing = introns out, exons joined.**
