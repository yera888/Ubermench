**What it is:** a DNA “volume knob” that increases transcription. They are **optional regulatory elements** that help tune **when/where/how much** a gene is expressed, often in multicellular eukaryotes.

Most genes have enhancers except for housekeeping genes.

**Where is it?**

- Can be far: upstream, downstream, inside introns, etc.
    
- Works by DNA looping so it can contact the [[Promoter]].
    

A- DNA looping: An enhancer can be far away in linear DNA but still touch the promoter in 3D because DNA loops.

B-same “regulatory neighborhood”: Genes are organized into neighborhoods (often called domains). Interactions mostly happen **within the same neighborhood**, and drop off **across boundaries**.

C- correct proteins are present: Even if it’s nearby, it won’t do anything unless the right **transcription factors/repressors** are present in that cell type.

**What does it do?**

- Binds [[Transcription factor]]s that help boost [[Transcription]] at a target promoter.
