### Enzyme 

They speed up processes; break down biomolecules 

>Pac-man = Most Protein
>Pac-mans mouth - active site + 
   Pebbles - substrates =
   Enzyme- substrate complex

- end in **-ase** 

The active site then changes shape to fit it perfectly = induced fit 

EX: Lactose(substrate) cant be broken down without enough lactase. 

- Lipase breaks down lipids (small intestine)
- Protease, pepsin (stomach) breaks down proteins into peptides; and trypsin (small intestine) 
- Amylase breaks down starch (in the mouth)
- Nucleases break down nucleic acids (DNA, RNA) breaking them into nucleotides 

And then they are broken down into even more. They can even be found in viruses. 

Synthesis and digestion rely heavily on enzymes for completion. 
#### Catalyst 

>Catalysts is a substance that increases the rate of a chemical reaction without itself undergoing any permanent chemical change. This means it can be used over and over again. 

Enzymes get help called **cofactors** (zinc + iron) and **coenzymes** (vitamins). They can be permanent or temporary 

Sometimes without the coenzymes or cofactors the enzyme wont work at all. 

Enzymes like acidic PH, different enzymes have ideal PH and temperature and they become denatured and cant work correctly when this PH or temperature changes.
