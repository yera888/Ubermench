When [[bone]] meets bone *arthrology*. The place of contact between bone, between bone and cartilage or bone and teeth.

>Bones *articulate* with each other at a joint.

More specifically you have the [[Skeletal System]] & [[Muscular System]].

When the skeleton interacts with the muscular system the skeleton helps the body move. Bones will meet with joints since bones themselves are too rigid to move alone.

## Classification of Joints

---
###### Joints are classified by their connective tissue and if there is a space between the articulating bones. This is the Structural Category.
##### [[Fibrous Joint]]:
Where bones are held together by dense regular (fibrous) connective tissue; no joint cavity.
##### [[Cartilaginous Joint]]: 
Where bones are jointed by cartilage; no joint cavity.

##### [[Synovial Joint]]:
fluid-filled joint cavity separating the articulating surfaces of the bone.

#### In most cases one end of a muscle is attached to an immovable or fixed part on one side of a joint. and the other end of a muscle connects to a movable part at the opposite side of the joint. 

What *contraction* is is when the muscle exerts a pull on the moveable end and movement occurs at the joint.
