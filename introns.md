the cut out.

A gene is a movie script with commercial breaks in parts of the movie. (non-coding parts).

These long stretches of DNA do not have the instructions for making proteins. 

+ much longer than exons about 90%

After a cell transcribes a gene, it cuts out the introns from the RNA in a process known as [[RNA splicing]].

If these cells were not removed, the movie wouldn't make any sense. If they were left in the ribosome would read them as if they were instructions adding thousands of random amino acids into the middle of a protein causing it to fold incorrectly. 

It also maintains the reading frame. They used to be thought of as "junk DNA" until it was discovered that.

[[alternative splicing]]
The cell can choose to skip certain exons or glue them in different orders so one gene can make many different proteins. 

2.
[[gene regulation]] 
Introns usually have switches that tell the cell when to turn a gene on or off or how much protein to make.

Random mutations or damage to DNA will most likely hit an intron instead of a vital exon since it's so much larger. So it protects the genetic instructions.
