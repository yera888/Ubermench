**The most common joint**
The articulating surfaces are enclosed within a capsule and the bones are joined by various ligaments. Bone in the synovial joint is separated by a joint cavity.

Have multiple [[sensory nerves]] -> which detect the amount of stretch or compression, the nervous system can detect changes in our posture and adjust body movements. Painful stimuli are transmitted through these sensory nerves when structures in the joints are stretched or compressed beyond normal limits or if there is [[inflammation]]. 


AND [[blood vessels]] that supply the articular capsule and associated [[ligaments]].

The *joint cavity* is lined by [[synovial membrane]] .

![[Pasted image 20260217191542.png]]
All common synovial joints have distinguishing features like [[articular capsule]], joint cavity, [[synovial fluid]] (only a small amount), articular cartilage, ligaments, nerves and blood vessels. 

Accessory structures are: [[bursa]], [[fat pads]] and [[tendons]].

All articulating bone surfaces in this joint is covered by a thin layer of [[hyaline cartilage]] named [[articular cartilage]].

Movement of a bone at a synovial joint is described with respect to 3 [[perpendicular]] planes or axes:

-[[uniaxial]]
-[[biaxial]]
-[[multiaxial]]
