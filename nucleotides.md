Each nucleotide has:

- a **sugar** (deoxyribose)
    
- a **phosphate**
    
- a **base**: **A, C, G, T**

On these nucleotide stands. Each base on a strand binds weakly to a base on the other strand, holding the two together. This pairing follows two simple yet powerful rules: adenine can bind only with thymine, and guanine can bind only with cytosine.

A -> T (AT&T)

G -> C (Guildford County)
