This fluid is composed of secretions from synovial membrane cells and a filtrate from blood plasma. 

>Permits separation of the articulating bones. 

FUNCTIONS for the [[Synovial joint]]

**-Lubricate**
Lubricates the [[articular cartilage]] on the articulating bones 

> [!quote] Similar to
> The way oil in a car engine lubricates the moving engine parts.

**-Nourish the chondrocyte**
The synovial fluid needs to be circulated continually to provide nutrients and remove wastes to these cells. When movement occurs at this joint the combined compression and re-expansion of the articular cartilage circulate the synovial fluid into and out the cartilage matrix. 

**-Act as a shock absorber**
It distributes stress and force evenly across the articular surfaces when the pressure in the joint suddenly increases.
