- **Promoters**: right near a gene’s start; where RNA polymerase and “basic” transcription machinery bind.
    
- **Enhancers**: can be far away (upstream, downstream, even in introns); boost transcription by helping recruit/activate transcription machinery.
    
- **Silencers**: reduce transcription.
    
- **Insulators / boundary elements**: block an enhancer from affecting the “wrong” gene, help define regulatory neighborhoods.
    
- **Response elements**: specific short sequences that bind factors like hormone receptors (often enhancer-like).
