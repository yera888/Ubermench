In humans, the average gene can be spliced to form one of six different proteins.  But some genes can generate thousands of different proteins, each one of them serving a function of its own. That is why humans can be so complex, even though we only have about 20,000 genes.
### Alternative splicing (the “choose different versions” idea)

- The cell can splice the **same pre-mRNA in different ways**.
    
- That produces **different mature mRNAs** from the same gene.
    
- Those different mRNAs can make **different protein versions** (or sometimes an RNA that gets degraded / acts differently).
    

So: **alternative splicing = different exon combinations → different outputs.** **Alternative splicing** is when a gene has **more than one splicing pattern**, letting one gene produce multiple products.
