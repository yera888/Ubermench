Transport dietary lipids, larger lipids and lipid-soluble vitamins cant enter the blood from the GI tract instead they transport through [[lacteals]] which drain inter larger lymphatic vessels and into the circulatory system. 

Size smallest to largest:

(microscopic vessels)
[[lymphatic capillaries]]:
They are intertwined with most blood capillaries. 


[[lymphatic vessels]]:
Are similar to small veins because they contain intima, media and extra and have valves in their lumen.

Afferent lymphatic vessels transport lymph directly to lymphoid organs called lymph nodes where it filters for foreign antigens.  Once filtered the lymph exits via efferent lymphatic vessels. 



[[lymphatic trunks]]:
These form from merging lymphatic vessels. 



[[lymphatic ducts]]: 
This is where lymphatic trunks dump into. There are 2 and they empty lymph back into the venous circulation.
