
These genes are still “real genes,” but their final product is an **RNA that functions as RNA** (no protein made). Main types:

- **rRNA** (ribosomal RNA): structural/catalytic core of the ribosome (needed to make proteins)
    
- **tRNA** (transfer RNA): adaptor that brings amino acids to the ribosome during translation
    
- **snRNA** (small nuclear RNA): helps splice pre-mRNA (removes introns)
    
- **snoRNA** (small nucleolar RNA): helps process/modify rRNA
- 
- **miRNA** (microRNA): short RNAs that reduce expression of target mRNAs (gene regulation)
    
- **siRNA** (small interfering RNA): guides mRNA destruction (often defense/regulation)
    
- **lncRNA** (long noncoding RNA): many roles in regulating transcription/chromatin/scaffolding
    
- (Sometimes included) **circRNA**: circular RNAs with regulatory roles
    

Also: some RNAs are made from genes but aren’t neatly “one gene → one RNA” (lots of complexity), but the above is the standard toolkit.
