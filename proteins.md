An essential macromolecule for all known forms of life. Proteins are three-dimensional biological polymers constructed from a set of 20 different monomers called amino acids.

+ They give cells structure and carry out many chemical reactions essential for life. 
+ Some can act as [[enzymes]].
+  Like the proteins that break down food some can carry important molecules like hemoglobin.
+  They can deliver information, relaying signals within a cell or between cells.

There is an estimated 100,000 kinds of protein made in the human body. 

All are made out of the same building blocks -> [[amino acids]] 

Hemoglobin is a ->[[ Quaternary Structure ]] which is in a shape that enables it to carry oxygen in red blood cells. 

The information for how to assemble the sequence for that structure is in the [[DNA]].
